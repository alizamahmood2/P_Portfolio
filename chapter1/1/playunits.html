Chapter 1: Industrial Design / Playground Units

Solo Studio Project
Islamabad, Pakistan
Product, Installation
Feb - June 2018

Playground units designed for open-ended free play for children's playgrounds in Islamabad, Pakistan.

This project aims to transform the existing neighbourhood children’s playgrounds in Islamabad to provide an open-ended space for free play.The final design consists of multiple modules that can be assembled in different configurations to create variations of play spaces.





Research

EXISTING PROBLEMS:

* No levels of difficulty
* Short duration of occupancy
* Does not afford risk-taking
* Rusted and broken
* Under-maintained
* Unsuccessful interactions
* Only physical play

I conducted observation studies at 20 different parks and playgrounds in Islamabad, Pakistan. They all had the same generic outdated equipment. This equipment had very few short durations of occupancy across all studies. They didn't allow multiple types of play and lacked challenging or creative play opportunities.





Ideation

I explored different modular forms and possibilities, starting from very basic rectangular designs to then pivoting towards a more organic free flowing form. I also considered the possibility of making the equipment responsive, but left it out of the final design due to high costs.





Final Design

Due to its modular design, it can be installated in a variety of different compositions at different playgrounds across the city.
