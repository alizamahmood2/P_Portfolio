Chapter 1: Industrial Design / Chair

Solo Project
Islamabad, Pakistan
Furniture, Product
March 2016

A chair inspired by the Dancing House by Frank Gehry.

Photographed by Aliza Mahmood

I contructed this chair out of Mohoghany Wood using hand saws and chisels (no power tools). The surface of the wood was sanded extensively by hand, then covered in two layers of clear polish, followed by a single layer of lacquer.

Cost of wood = PKR 4,400

Joinery: Wood glue is used in 4/9 joints only for reinforcement. The rest of the joints have no adhesives or joinery.

I started off by studying and deconstructing the building in layers - exploring the aesthetic, compositional and conceptual elements of the design. The final design for the chair aimed to harmoniously merge straight lines with gradual curves to achieve a stable form. The chair is made up of 9 pieces of Mahogany Wood, all connected to each other through Mortise and Tenon joints.

Senam Khan, a fellow designer helped me construct the chair and sand it.
